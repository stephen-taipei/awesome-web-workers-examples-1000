About Fourier Series

Fourier series represent periodic functions as infinite sums of sines and cosines. Any periodic function can be expressed as a combination of harmonic frequencies.

Fourier Series Formula

f(x) = a₀/2 + Σₙ₌₁^∞ [aₙcos(nx) + bₙsin(nx)]

Fourier Coefficients

DC Component (a₀)
a₀ = (1/π) ∫₋π^π f(x) dx
Cosine Coefficients (aₙ)
aₙ = (1/π) ∫₋π^π f(x)cos(nx) dx
Sine Coefficients (bₙ)
bₙ = (1/π) ∫₋π^π f(x)sin(nx) dx

Convergence Properties

  • Pointwise: Converges at continuous points
  • Gibbs phenomenon: ~9% overshoot at discontinuities
  • Parseval's theorem: Energy in time = energy in frequency
  • Even functions: Only cosine terms (bₙ = 0)
  • Odd functions: Only sine terms (aₙ = 0)
