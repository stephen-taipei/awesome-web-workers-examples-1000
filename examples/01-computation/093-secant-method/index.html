About Secant Method

The Secant Method is a root-finding algorithm that uses a secant line (through two points) to approximate the root, avoiding the need for derivative computation.

Algorithm

x_{n+1} = x_n - f(x_n) * (x_n - x_{n-1}) / (f(x_n) - f(x_{n-1}))

Properties

PropertyValue
ConvergenceSuperlinear (order ≈ 1.618)
Orderφ = (1 + √5) / 2 ≈ 1.618 (Golden ratio)
RequirementTwo initial guesses
DerivativeNot required

Comparison with Newton's Method

  • Pro: No derivative computation needed
  • Pro: Simpler implementation
  • Con: Slightly slower convergence (1.618 vs 2)
  • Con: Needs two starting points

Comparison with Bisection

  • Pro: Much faster (superlinear vs linear)
  • Con: Can diverge (no guaranteed convergence)
  • Con: Doesn't require bracket with sign change
