About Genetic Algorithm

Genetic Algorithms (GA) are optimization methods inspired by natural selection. A population of candidate solutions evolves through selection, crossover, and mutation to find optimal or near-optimal solutions.

Algorithm:

1. Initialize: Generate random population of N individuals
2. Evaluate: Calculate fitness f(x) for each individual
3. Selection: Choose parents based on fitness (tournament, roulette wheel)
4. Crossover: Combine parents to create offspring
   - Single-point: [a₁...aₖ|aₖ₊₁...aₙ] × [b₁...bₖ|bₖ₊₁...bₙ] → [a₁...aₖ|bₖ₊₁...bₙ]
   - BLX-α: child = parent₁ + α(parent₂ - parent₁) × random
5. Mutation: Random perturbation with probability p_m
6. Replacement: Form new generation
7. Repeat steps 2-6 until convergence

Key Parameters:
  Population size:    N = 50-200
  Crossover rate:     p_c = 0.6-0.9
  Mutation rate:      p_m = 0.01-0.1
  Selection pressure: Controls diversity vs convergence
