About Gradient Computation

The gradient ∇f is a vector of all partial derivatives, pointing in the direction of steepest ascent.

Numerical Gradient Methods

Forward Difference
∂f/∂xᵢ ≈ [f(x + heᵢ) - f(x)] / h
O(h)
Central Difference
∂f/∂xᵢ ≈ [f(x + heᵢ) - f(x - heᵢ)] / 2h
O(h²)
Backward Difference
∂f/∂xᵢ ≈ [f(x) - f(x - heᵢ)] / h
O(h)

Test Functions

  • Sphere: Convex, bowl-shaped, minimum at origin
  • Rosenbrock: Non-convex "banana valley", minimum at (1,1,...)
  • Rastrigin: Highly multi-modal with many local minima
  • Ackley: Nearly flat outer region with deep hole at origin

Applications

  • Optimization: Gradient descent, conjugate gradient methods
  • Machine Learning: Backpropagation, training neural networks
  • Physics: Force fields, potential energy minimization
