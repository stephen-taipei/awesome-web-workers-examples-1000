About Boundary Value Problems

Boundary Value Problems (BVPs) are differential equations with conditions specified at two or more points, unlike initial value problems where all conditions are at one point.

General Form

y'' = f(x, y, y') with y(a) = α and y(b) = β

Solution Methods

MethodDescriptionPros/Cons
ShootingConvert to IVP, iterate on initial slopeSimple, but may not converge
Finite DifferenceDiscretize derivatives, solve linear systemRobust, direct solution
CollocationPolynomial approximation at selected pointsHigh accuracy, smooth solution
RelaxationIterative refinement of guessGood for nonlinear problems

Applications

  • Beam deflection and structural analysis
  • Heat conduction with fixed temperatures
  • Electrostatics with boundary potentials
  • Quantum mechanics (Schrödinger equation)
