About Polynomial Root Finding

The Durand-Kerner method (also known as Weierstrass method) finds all roots of a polynomial simultaneously, including complex roots.

Algorithm

z_k^{new} = z_k - P(z_k) / ∏_{j≠k}(z_k - z_j)

Each root estimate is updated using the current polynomial value and the distances to all other root estimates.

Properties

PropertyValue
ConvergenceQuadratic (near simple roots)
FindsAll n roots simultaneously
Complex rootsYes, automatically
Multiple rootsSlower convergence

Key Features

  • Simultaneous: All roots converge at the same time
  • Complex: Naturally handles complex roots
  • Complete: Finds all n roots of degree-n polynomial
  • Parallel: Each root can be updated independently

Comparison with Other Methods

  • vs Newton: Finds all roots at once vs one at a time
  • vs Companion Matrix: Iterative vs direct eigenvalue
  • vs Laguerre: Simpler but may be slower
