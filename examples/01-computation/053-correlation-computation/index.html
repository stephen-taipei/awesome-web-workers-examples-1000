About Correlation

Correlation measures the similarity between signals or the relationship between variables. It's fundamental to signal processing, statistics, and time series analysis.

Methods

Method Description
Autocorrelation Correlation of signal with itself at different lags
Cross-Correlation Similarity between two signals at different time shifts
Pearson Linear relationship between two variables (-1 to +1)
Time Delay Find lag where signals are most aligned
Correlogram ACF/PACF for time series analysis

Interpretation

Value Meaning
r = 1.0 Perfect positive correlation
r = 0.7-0.9 Strong positive correlation
r = 0.4-0.6 Moderate correlation
r ~ 0 No linear relationship
r = -1.0 Perfect negative correlation
