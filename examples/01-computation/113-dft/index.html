About Discrete Fourier Transform

The DFT converts a finite sequence of samples from the time domain into the frequency domain, revealing the frequency components present in the signal.

DFT Formula

X[k] = Σₙ₌₀^(N-1) x[n] · e^(-i·2πkn/N)
where k = 0, 1, ..., N-1

Euler's Formula Expansion

Complex Exponential
e^(-iθ) = cos(θ) - i·sin(θ)
Real Part
Re(X[k]) = Σ x[n]·cos(2πkn/N)
Imaginary Part
Im(X[k]) = -Σ x[n]·sin(2πkn/N)

Key Properties

  • Magnitude: |X[k]| = √(Re² + Im²) - strength at frequency k
  • Phase: ∠X[k] = atan2(Im, Re) - phase shift
  • Symmetry: For real signals, X[N-k] = X[k]*
  • Parseval's theorem: Energy in time = energy in frequency
  • Frequency resolution: Δf = fs/N (sampling freq / samples)
