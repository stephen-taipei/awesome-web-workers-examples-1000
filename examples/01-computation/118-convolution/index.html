About Convolution

Convolution combines two signals to produce a third signal. It's fundamental to filtering, smoothing, edge detection, and many signal processing applications.

Convolution Formula

(f * g)[n] = Σₖ f[k] · g[n - k]
Output is sum of weighted, shifted copies of the kernel

FFT Convolution

Time Domain
y = x * h
Frequency Domain
Y = X · H
Convolution Theorem
x * h = IFFT(FFT(x) · FFT(h))

Common Kernels

  • Low-pass (Box): [1/n, 1/n, ..., 1/n] - Smoothing/averaging
  • Gaussian: Bell curve - Natural smoothing
  • High-pass: [-1, 2, -1] - Edge detection
  • Derivative: [-1, 0, 1] - Rate of change
  • Laplacian: [1, -2, 1] - Second derivative

Complexity

  • Direct: O(N × M) where N = signal length, M = kernel length
  • FFT: O((N+M) log(N+M)) - Much faster for large kernels
