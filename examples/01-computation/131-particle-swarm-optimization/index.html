About PSO

Particle Swarm Optimization (PSO) is a computational method that optimizes a problem by iteratively improving candidate solutions based on the collective behavior of particles (similar to bird flocking or fish schooling).

Algorithm:

For each particle i:
  v_i(t+1) = w × v_i(t)                           (Inertia)
           + c₁ × r₁ × (p_best_i - x_i(t))        (Cognitive)
           + c₂ × r₂ × (g_best - x_i(t))          (Social)

  x_i(t+1) = x_i(t) + v_i(t+1)                    (Position update)

Parameters:
  w  = Inertia weight (0.4 - 0.9)
  c₁ = Cognitive coefficient (1.5 - 2.0)
  c₂ = Social coefficient (1.5 - 2.0)
  r₁, r₂ = Random values in [0, 1]

Variants:
  - Standard PSO: Fixed parameters
  - LDWPSO: Linearly decreasing inertia weight
  - Constriction PSO: χ-factor for convergence
