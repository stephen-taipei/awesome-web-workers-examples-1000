About Cross-Correlation

Cross-correlation measures the similarity between two signals as a function of time lag. It's used for pattern matching, time delay estimation, and signal detection.

Cross-Correlation Formula

(x ⋆ y)[n] = Σₖ x[k] · y[k + n]
Unlike convolution, no time reversal is applied

FFT-Based Computation

Correlation Theorem
x ⋆ y = IFFT(X* · Y)
X* = conjugate
X*[k] = Re(X[k]) - j·Im(X[k])

Types of Correlation

  • Cross-correlation: Similarity between two different signals
  • Auto-correlation: Signal correlated with itself - reveals periodicity
  • Normalized: Scaled to [-1, 1] range for comparison

Applications

  • Time delay estimation: Find lag where correlation peaks
  • Pattern matching: Locate template in larger signal
  • Periodicity detection: Auto-correlation shows repeating patterns
  • Signal detection: Find known signal in noise
